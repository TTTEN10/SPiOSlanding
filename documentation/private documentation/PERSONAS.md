# SafePsy — Personas

This file lists **user personas**, **partners**, and **investors** for messaging, pitch, and partnership materials.

- **Tailored explanations:** [LAUNCH.md](./LAUNCH.md#explanations-by-persona) — SafePsy explained for each persona (users, partners, investors).
- **Approved wording:** [CLAIMS_AND_TRUST_LANGUAGE.md](../public documentation/CLAIMS_AND_TRUST_LANGUAGE.md) — use for any public claim.

---

## 1. User personas (end users)

| Persona | Description | Main need / motivation |
|--------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **In-therapy** | Currently in licensed therapy; wants support between sessions. | Private space to reflect, prepare for appointments, practice coping tools; no extra data trail. |
| **Considering therapy** | Not yet in therapy; exploring or hesitant. | Low-pressure, confidential entry point; organize thoughts and emotions without commitment. |
| **Privacy-first** | Cares strongly about data ownership and control. | No training on their data, no third-party storage; keys in their control (wallet). |
| **First-step seeker** | Stigma or access concerns; not ready to book a therapist. | Anonymous, wallet-based; no centralized account linking identity to mental health. |
| **Global / access** | Limited local access to therapy or prefers digital-first. | Borderless access; transparent pricing; privacy by design. |
| **Tech-comfortable** | Already uses Web3/crypto or is willing to try. | Wallet + DID is a feature (sovereignty), not a barrier. |
| **Skeptical of AI therapy** | Worried AI will replace or mislead. | Clear positioning: AI complements therapy, does not diagnose or treat. |

## 2. Partners

### Psychology associations & professional bodies

| Type | Examples (illustrative) | What they care about |
|------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| **National psychology associations** | APA (US), national equivalents (e.g. EFPA members) | Ethics, scope of practice, no substitution for licensed care; alignment with guidelines. |
| **Regulatory / licensing bodies** | State boards, professional colleges | No unlicensed practice; clear boundaries of AI vs. therapist. |
| **Training / education** | Universities, continuing education providers | How future practitioners and patients use digital tools; ethics and privacy. |

### Organizations & institutions

| Type | Examples (illustrative) | What they care about |
|------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| **Healthcare systems / payers** | Hospitals, insurers, public health (non-US) | Privacy, compliance (GDPR etc.), complement to (not replacement of) care. |
| **Employer wellness** | EAPs, HR wellness programs | Privacy for employees; no data shared with employer; scalable support. |
| **Research** | Universities, clinical research units | Data governance, consent, potential for studies without central data hoarding. |

### NGOs & civil society

| Type | Examples (illustrative) | What they care about |
|------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| **Mental health NGOs** | Advocacy, crisis lines, peer support networks | Access, stigma reduction, safety (no replacement for crisis care). |
| **Digital rights / privacy NGOs** | EFF, local privacy orgs | User control, no surveillance, transparency, open governance where relevant. |
| **Humanitarian / global health** | NGOs in low-resource or conflict settings | Access without dependency on central servers; resilience; pricing. |

## 3. Investors

### By type

| Type | Description | Typical focus |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Solo / angel investor** | Individual; often founder or operator background. | Problem–solution fit, team, traction; sometimes mission (mental health, privacy). |
| **Incubator / accelerator** | Program with mentorship, sometimes small check. | Product–market fit, scalability, governance; cohort and network. |
| **Venture capital (VC)** | Fund; Seed to Series A/B. | TAM, unit economics, defensibility, regulatory path; B2B2C or B2C potential. |
| **Centralized exchange (CEX)** | Listing / investment arm of a CEX. | Tokenomics, liquidity, compliance; user base overlap with crypto users. |
| **Decentralized exchange / DeFi** | DEX, protocol, or DAO treasury. | Token utility, on-chain identity (DID), governance; alignment with “sovereignty” narrative. |

### By thesis (overlap with above)

| Thesis | What they look for |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Health tech / digital health** | Clinical relevance, compliance, distribution (payers, providers). |
| **Web3 / blockchain** | Real use of chain (DID, governance); sustainable token model. |
| **Privacy / security** | Technical and policy credibility; no overclaim (see CLAIMS_AND_TRUST_LANGUAGE). |
| **Impact / ESG** | Mental health access, equity, measurable impact. |

## How to use this file

- **Messaging:** Use [LAUNCH.md](./LAUNCH.md) “Explanations by persona” for one-pagers, emails, and slide decks.
- **Partnerships:** Lead with the partner’s priorities (ethics, access, privacy, research) and use approved trust language.
- **Investors:** Lead with their type and thesis (health tech vs. Web3 vs. impact); avoid overclaiming compliance or “end-to-end” encryption.
