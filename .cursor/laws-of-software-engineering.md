# Laws of Software Engineering (reference)

Source: [Laws of Software Engineering](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/) by Dr. Milan Milanović.

This file is a **reference list** (not a Cursor rule) of the laws collected on the site.

## Laws (56)

- **Conway's Law**: Organizations design systems that mirror their own communication structure. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/conways-law/))
- **Premature Optimization (Knuth's Optimization Principle)**: Premature optimization is the root of all evil. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/premature-optimization/))
- **Hyrum's Law**: With a sufficient number of API users, all observable behaviors of your system will be depended on by somebody. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/hyrums-law/))
- **The Boy Scout Rule**: Leave the code better than you found it. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/boy-scout-rule/))
- **YAGNI (You Aren't Gonna Need It)**: Don't add functionality until it is necessary. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/yagni/))
- **Brooks's Law**: Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/brooks-law/))
- **Gall's Law**: A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/galls-law/))
- **The Law of Leaky Abstractions**: All non-trivial abstractions, to some degree, are leaky. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/law-of-leaky-abstractions/))
- **Tesler's Law (Conservation of Complexity)**: Every application has an inherent amount of irreducible complexity that can only be shifted, not eliminated. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/teslers-law/))
- **CAP Theorem**: A distributed system can guarantee only two of: consistency, availability, and partition tolerance. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/cap-theorem/))
- **Second-System Effect**: Small, successful systems tend to be followed by overengineered, bloated replacements. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/second-system-effect/))
- **Fallacies of Distributed Computing**: A set of eight false assumptions that new distributed system designers often make. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/fallacies-of-distributed-computing/))
- **Law of Unintended Consequences**: Whenever you change a complex system, expect surprise. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/law-of-unintended-consequences/))
- **Zawinski's Law**: Every program attempts to expand until it can read mail. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/zawinskis-law/))
- **Dunbar's Number**: There is a cognitive limit of about 150 stable relationships one person can maintain. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/dunbars-number/))
- **The Ringelmann Effect**: Individual productivity decreases as group size increases. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/ringelmann-effect/))
- **Price's Law**: The square root of the total number of participants does 50% of the work. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/prices-law/))
- **Putt's Law**: Those who understand technology don't manage it, and those who manage it don't understand it. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/putts-law/))
- **Peter Principle**: In a hierarchy, every employee tends to rise to their level of incompetence. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/peter-principle/))
- **Bus Factor**: The minimum number of team members whose loss would put the project in serious trouble. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/bus-factor/))
- **Dilbert Principle**: Companies tend to promote incompetent employees to management to limit the damage they can do. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/dilbert-principle/))
- **Parkinson's Law**: Work expands to fill the time available for its completion. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/parkinsons-law/))
- **The Ninety-Ninety Rule**: The first 90% of the code accounts for the first 90% of development time; the remaining 10% accounts for the other 90%. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/ninety-ninety-rule/))
- **Hofstadter's Law**: It always takes longer than you expect, even when you take into account Hofstadter's Law. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/hofstadters-law/))
- **Goodhart's Law**: When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/goodharts-law/))
- **Gilb's Law**: Anything you need to quantify can be measured in some way better than not measuring it. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/gilbs-law/))
- **Murphy's Law / Sod's Law**: Anything that can go wrong will go wrong. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/murphys-law/))
- **Postel's Law**: Be conservative in what you do, be liberal in what you accept from others. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/postels-law/))
- **Broken Windows Theory**: Don't leave broken windows (bad designs, wrong decisions, or poor code) unrepaired. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/broken-windows-theory/))
- **Technical Debt**: Technical Debt is everything that slows us down when developing software. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/technical-debt/))
- **Linus's Law**: Given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/linuss-law/))
- **Kernighan's Law**: Debugging is twice as hard as writing the code in the first place. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/kernighans-law/))
- **Testing Pyramid**: A project should have many fast unit tests, fewer integration tests, and only a small number of UI tests. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/testing-pyramid/))
- **Pesticide Paradox**: Repeatedly running the same tests becomes less effective over time. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/pesticide-paradox/))
- **Lehman's Laws of Software Evolution**: Software that reflects the real world must evolve, and that evolution has predictable limits. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/lehmans-laws/))
- **Sturgeon's Law**: 90% of everything is crap. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/sturgeons-law/))
- **Amdahl's Law**: The speedup from parallelization is limited by the fraction of work that cannot be parallelized. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/amdahls-law/))
- **Gustafson's Law**: It is possible to achieve significant speedup in parallel processing by increasing the problem size. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/gustafsons-law/))
- **Metcalfe's Law**: The value of a network is proportional to the square of the number of users. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/metcalfes-law/))
- **DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself)**: Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous, authoritative representation. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/dry-principle/))
- **KISS (Keep It Simple, Stupid)**: Designs and systems should be as simple as possible. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/kiss-principle/))
- **SOLID Principles**: Five main guidelines that enhance software design, making code more maintainable and scalable. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/solid-principles/))
- **Law of Demeter**: An object should only interact with its immediate friends, not strangers. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/law-of-demeter/))
- **Principle of Least Astonishment**: Software and interfaces should behave in a way that least surprises users and other developers. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/principle-of-least-astonishment/))
- **Dunning-Kruger Effect**: The less you know about something, the more confident you tend to be. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/dunning-kruger-effect/))
- **Hanlon's Razor**: Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by stupidity or carelessness. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/hanlons-razor/))
- **Occam's Razor**: The simplest explanation is often the most accurate one. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/occams-razor/))
- **Sunk Cost Fallacy**: Sticking with a choice because you've invested time or energy in it, even when walking away helps you. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/sunk-cost-fallacy/))
- **The Map Is Not the Territory**: Our representations of reality are not the same as reality itself. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/map-is-not-the-territory/))
- **Confirmation Bias**: A tendency to favor information that supports our existing beliefs or ideas. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/confirmation-bias/))
- **The Hype Cycle & Amara's Law**: We tend to overestimate the effect of a technology in the short run and underestimate the impact in the long run. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/hype-cycle-amaras-law/))
- **The Lindy Effect**: The longer something has been in use, the more likely it is to continue being used. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/lindy-effect/))
- **First Principles Thinking**: Breaking a complex problem into its most basic blocks and then building up from there. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/first-principles-thinking/))
- **Inversion**: Solving a problem by considering the opposite outcome and working backward from it. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/inversion/))
- **Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)**: 80% of the problems result from 20% of the causes. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/pareto-principle/))
- **Cunningham's Law**: The best way to get the correct answer on the Internet is not to ask a question, it's to post the wrong answer. ([details](https://lawsofsoftwareengineering.com/laws/cunninghams-law/))
